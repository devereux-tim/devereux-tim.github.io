Projects

Remote Sensing of Vegetation Structure with Orbital Lidar

My honours research was centred on testing the efficacy of measuring vegetation structural metrics using novel orbital LiDAR waveforms and ultimately developing automated workflows to do so at massive scales. Lidar derived measurements were processed and compared with Queensland Government BioCondition assessment data in a range of regional ecosystems over the entire state using high performance computing. This research was successful in securing a competitive scholarship from the Queensland Resource Council's mine site rehabilitation fund.

Automatic individual tree extraction from terrestrial LiDAR point clouds.

This research project involved the testing of new tree extraction algorithms on terrestrial LiDAR point clouds obtained in two structurally contrasting forest plots. I was able to achieve segmentationof 81% of trees present and also improved efficiency of the software by implementing parallelisation.The results generated from this study help to define the limitations of a novel tree extraction algorithmwithin varying forest types in the Australian setting.

IOT Weather Monitor

Weather sensors linked to a microcontroller which uploads data to Thinkspeak, a free IOT platfrom provided by MathWorks using a script written in C. Temperature, humidity, windspeed, rainfall and baromatric pressure are retrieved and displayed on a simple webpage using Chart.js. The sensors are powered by a small solar panel which stores energy in a 2500mAh LiPo battery. The C code was developed for optimal power efficency of the sensors and transmission of data to ensure uninterrupted function.
